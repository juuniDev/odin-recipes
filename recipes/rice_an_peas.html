Rice an' Peas

Description

In my Jamaican home, the kitchen was our heartbeat, where flavors united our family. The previous Jamaican Rice and Peas version here missed the mark on the true cultural essence. Today, I proudly share my mom's recipe.

Jamaican rice and peas, a soulful creation with thyme, scallion, and garlic, embodies the island's spirit. Picture Sundays, the kitchen alive with this masterpiece and baked chicken. Earthy notes from pimento seeds and a gentle warmth from Scotch bonnet pepper define its charm. Scotch bonnet peppers, a constant in our kitchen, add a subtle, fruity undertone. Use them whole for a milder heat. Habanero pepper works as a substitute.

Gungo peas (or pigeon peas) are used in this recipe but red kidney beans are a good substitute. The coconut milk, tinged with bean hues, gives the rice its signature blush.

For a taste of authentic Jamaican rice and peas, here's the cherished recipe:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried gungo peas, rinsed, soaked overnight, and drained
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 5 allspice berries
  • 5 thyme sprigs
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 (13.5-ounce) can unsweetened coconut milk
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice

Steps

  1. Gather your ingredients.
  2. In a large saucepan, cover the beans with the water and bring to a boil over moderately high heat. Stir in the onion, scallions, garlic, allspice, thyme, Scotch bonnet, ginger, 2 teaspoons salt, and 1/2 teaspoon pepper.
  3. Stir in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  4. Cover and simmer over low heat until the beans are tender, about 1 hour; adjust the heat as necessary to maintain a gentle simmer.
  5. Stir in the rice; cover and simmer over low heat until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed, about 30 minutes.
  6. Remove from the heat and let steam for 10 minutes, then discard the thyme stems, allspice berries, and Scotch bonnet. Using a fork, fluff the rice and beans and season with salt.
  7. Serve hot.
