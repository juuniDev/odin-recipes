Description

In my Jamaican home, the kitchen was our heartbeat, where flavors united our family. The previous Jamaican Rice and Peas version here missed the mark on the true cultural essence. Today, I proudly share my mom's recipe.

Jamaican rice and peas, a soulful creation with thyme, scallion, and garlic, embodies the island's spirit. Picture Sundays, the kitchen alive with this masterpiece and baked chicken. Earthy notes from pimento seeds and a gentle warmth from Scotch bonnet pepper define its charm. Scotch bonnet peppers, a constant in our kitchen, add a subtle, fruity undertone. Use them whole for a milder heat. Habanero pepper works as a substitute.

Gungo peas "("Pigeon peas")" are used in this recipe but red kidney beans are a good substitute. The coconut milk, tinged with bean hues, gives the rice its signature blush.

For a taste of authentic Jamaican rice and peas, here's the cherished recipe.
