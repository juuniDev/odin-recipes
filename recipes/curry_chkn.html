Curry Chicken

Description

Americans like to think of America as a melting pot. But if they ever set foot on Trinidad and Tobago, they'd marvel at how so small a nation can be so vibrantly diverse. Curried chicken is a prime example.

Obviously, curry is an East Indian staple; and the chicken, rather than pork or beef, a nod to the many Hindu and Muslim Trinbagonians for whom those meats are haram, or forbidden. The rest of the ingredients I include-the culantro-heavy green seasoning, house spice from my mom's kitchen, and the omnipresent peppa sauce-round out the dish, while the potatoes add starchy balance and serve as repositories of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1½ cups Green Seasoning
  • 4 tablespoons Curry Powder
  • 1½ teaspoons House Spice
  • 4 chicken leg quarters
  • 2 tablespoons grapeseed oil, plus more as needed
  • 1 quart Chicken Stock
  • 1 red Scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 teaspoons Peppa Sauce
  • 3 large Yukon Gold potatoes (about 1½ pounds), peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes

Steps

  1. Combine the salt, onions, green seasoning, curry powder, house spice, and chicken in a large bowl. Cover and refrigerate for 24 hours.
  2. When ready to cook, add 2 tablespoons of oil to a large pot over high heat. When it shimmers, sear the chicken until deeply golden brown on both sides, about 4 minutes in total.
  3. Remove to a plate and set aside. Add ½ cup chicken stock to the pot, stirring to scrape up any browned bits, then return the chicken to the pan.
  4. Add any remaining marinade, the Scotch bonnet (leaving it whole), peppa sauce, and the rest of the chicken stock.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Simmer gently, uncovered, for 1 hour.
  6. Remove the chicken to a plate. Add the potatoes to the pot, cover partially, and simmer for 30 to 40 minutes, until just tender.
  7. Add up to ½ cup water as needed to keep saucy while the potatoes cook.
  8. Remove and discard the Scotch bonnet (being careful not to puncture it), then return the chicken to the pot.
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning, then serve with Perfectly Steamed Rice
  10. Curried chicken will keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days, or in the freezer for up to 2 months.
